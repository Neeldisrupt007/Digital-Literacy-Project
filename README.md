To create this infographic on Digital Literacy, I used Canva, a versatile graphic design tool that allows for a balance of custom illustrations and structured layouts. The platform’s extensive library of vector graphics was essential in maintaining the vibrant, modern aesthetic seen throughout the project.

Design Overview
The design is structured as a vertical flow, guiding the reader through three core pillars of the digital age:

Definition: Identifying digital literacy as the ability to find and evaluate online information.

Safety: Highlighting safe internet practices and providing official resources like the National Cyber Crime Portal.

Professionalism: Emphasizing the importance of a curated online presence and effective email communication.

I chose a high-contrast color palette of purple, pink, and teal to ensure the information feels engaging rather than academic. The "glitch" effect on the main title was a deliberate choice to visually represent the "digital" theme.

Reflections
The most interesting part of the process was visual storytelling—trying to represent abstract concepts like "evaluating information" through simple icons. I found it challenging to strike a balance between the artistic, overlapping elements and text readability. Ensuring that the background illustrations didn't distract from the crucial safety contact numbers required several iterations of layering and transparency adjustments.
